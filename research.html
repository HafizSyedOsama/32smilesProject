Connection Between Oral and Overall Health

Poor oral health can lead to serious medical conditions:
Systemic Diseases Linked to Oral Health

Heart Disease: Oral bacteria can enter the bloodstream, increasing heart disease risks.

Diabetes: Gum disease worsens blood sugar control, leading to complications.

Respiratory Problems: Inhaled bacteria from gum infections can cause pneumonia.

Pregnancy Risks: Poor oral hygiene increases the risk of preterm birth and low birth weight.

Daily Oral Hygiene Routine

  • Brush twice a day with fluoride toothpaste.

  • Floss daily to remove plaque between teeth.

  • Use an antibacterial mouthwash to kill germs.

  • Stay hydrated to maintain saliva production, which protects against bacteria.

Learn More

Healthy Diet for Strong Teeth

  • Eat calcium-rich foods (milk, cheese, yogurt) to strengthen enamel.

  • Avoid excess sugar that feeds cavity-causing bacteria.

  • Consume crunchy fruits and vegetables to naturally clean teeth.

Learn More

Regular Dental Visits for a Confident Smile

  • Get professional cleanings every six months.

  • Consult a dentist for teeth whitening if stains are an issue.

  • Consider orthodontic treatments for better alignment and an attractive smile.
